## Content overview


**Open Surgery vs Early Weight-Bearing: What’s the Best Achilles Strategy?**



A recent network meta-analysis covering **5,566 patients** compared open surgical repair, minimally invasive repair, and non-surgical treatment — combined with early vs. late weight-bearing protocols.


**Key takeaways:**



🔁 **Lowest re-rupture risk:** Open surgery + late weight-bearing (around 2%).


⏱️ **Speed vs risk trade-off:** Early weight-bearing gives faster return-to-sport and better patient satisfaction, but may slightly raise complications. 


⚠️ **Non-surgical care:** While re-rupture risk is higher, it remains a valid option for many when combined with structured rehab.



Clinically, this suggests the _optimal treatment plan depends on individual factors — not just “surgery or no surgery.”_


**Question:**


How do you decide when to recommend early weight-bearing — and how do you counsel patients on the potential risk-benefit balance?
