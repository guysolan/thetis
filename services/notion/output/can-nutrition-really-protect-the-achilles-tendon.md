## Content overview


Can Nutrition Really Protect the Achilles Tendon?


When we think of Achilles rupture prevention, we usually focus on training, footwear, or surgical techniques. But a growing body of research suggests that nutrition may play a bigger role than we realize in tendon resilience.


Here are some nutrients linked to tendon health:


🥗 **Vitamin C**


Essential for collagen synthesis the primary structural protein in tendons

- Food sources: citrus fruits, kiwi, berries, peppers
- Supplementation: often considered when intake is low or during periods of high collagen turnover

💪 **Collagen peptides**


May support tendon structure and recovery when combined with loading

- Food sources: bone broth, gelatin rich foods
- Supplementation: commonly used pre exercise in rehab settings, usually paired with vitamin C

⚡ **Amino acids especially glycine and proline**


Key building blocks of collagen

- Food sources: meat, fish, eggs, dairy
- Supplementation: generally unnecessary with adequate protein intake

🦵 **Vitamin D and Calcium**


Support bone tendon integration and neuromuscular function

- Food sources: oily fish, fortified dairy, sunlight for vitamin D
- Supplementation: considered in deficiency or low sun exposure populations

While diet alone won’t prevent a rupture, it can complement rehab and reduce vulnerability to overuse injuries. Some clinicians are now advocating for formal nutrition plans as part of Achilles recovery protocols.


The evidence is early but promising — and it may help close the gap between prevention and treatment.


Do you currently include nutrition guidance in your tendon injury protocols, or is this still overlooked in practice?
