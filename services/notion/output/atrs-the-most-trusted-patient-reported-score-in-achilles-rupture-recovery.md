## Content overview


**ATRS: The Most Trusted Patient-Reported Score in Achilles Rupture Recovery**



The **Achilles Tendon Total Rupture Score (ATRS)** is one of the most widely validated patient-reported outcome measures for Achilles tendon rupture. Developed in Sweden and adopted across Europe, Canada, the UK, and the US, it helps clinicians track functional recovery with accuracy and consistency.

📊 **What ATRS Measures:**
Patients rate symptoms and limitations such as:

- Walking ✔️
- Stairs ✔️
- Running & jumping ✔️
- Tendon stiffness & fatigue ✔️


Scores range from **0 to 100**, with higher scores indicating better function.

🔍 **Why ATRS Matters:**
Multiple validation studies show the ATRS is:

- 🎯 **Highly responsive** to changes during rehabilitation
- 📈 **Reliable for comparing** treatment strategies (operative vs non-operative)
- 🌍 **Useful in research** because it captures outcomes that matter to patients, not only clinicians


A 2021 review from the **University of Oxford** confirmed ATRS as one of the most sensitive tools for long-term follow-up after Achilles rupture.
