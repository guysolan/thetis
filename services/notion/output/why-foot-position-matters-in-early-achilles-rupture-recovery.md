## Why Foot Position Matters in Early Achilles Rupture Recovery


One of the simplest but most important concepts in early Achilles rupture care is tendon approximation.


When the foot is held in a neutral position, the torn tendon ends sit further apart.


When the foot is pointed down in plantarflexion, the ends move closer together, supporting healing.


Early recovery is not about movement progression.


It is about protection.


Small unprotected moments of dorsiflexion in the first weeks can influence tendon length and long term strength outcomes.


Clear patient education around positioning is essential, particularly in the emergency phase.


The goal early on is simple:


Keep the tendon ends close.


Keep them protected.
