## Content overview


Long-Term Deficits Hidden by High Scores! 😱

Are your Achilles Tendon Rupture (ATR) patients truly recovered? The answer might be **NO**! 🙅‍♂️ A major study following patients for **7 years** revealed a concerning disconnect.



### **The Key Details: Objective vs. Subjective** ⚖️

- 📅 **Follow-up:** Mean of 7 years post-ATR (Surgical & Non-Surgical).
- **Patient Score (ATRS):** Median score was a high **96/100**, suggesting minimal symptoms and full recovery. ✅
- **Objective Tests:** Despite feeling great, patients showed **continued, significant deficits** in calf muscle endurance and strength (e.g., heel-rise performance) that **did not improve** after the first 2 years! 📉

### **Clinical Insight** 💡


Don't let patient-reported outcomes (PROs) blind you to persistent biomechanical weakness! **Objective testing** (Limb Symmetry Index, strength dynamometry) remains crucial, even years after the injury, to prevent potential long-term issues or contralateral injury. 💪


**Question:
o you utilize objective LSI testing (heel-rise/dynamometry) before clearing ATR patients for high-level activities?** Tell us your criteria! 🤔
