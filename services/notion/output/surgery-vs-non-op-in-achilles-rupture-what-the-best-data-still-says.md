## Content overview


Surgery vs Non-Op in Achilles Rupture: What the Best Data Still Says



A comprehensive 2023 meta-analysis combining data from 33 studies (35,896 patients) compared surgical repair vs non-operative treatment for acute Achilles tendon rupture.


📉 **Re-rupture rate:** significantly lower with surgery (RR ~ 0.44) 


✅ **Return-to-sport rate:** ~14% higher in the surgical group 


⚠️ **Complication risk:** Infection and nerve injury were notably higher with surgery (infection ↑ RR 2.54, nerve injury ↑ RR 3.67) 🩹


Importantly — in studies that used **accelerated functional rehab + early range of motion**, the re-rupture difference between surgical and non-operative groups **was not significant**.


That suggests: when rehab is optimized, non-surgical management can approach surgical results — but surgical repair still reduces rerupture risk overall.



**Question for clinicians:** In your experience handling Achilles ruptures — do you lean more on surgery to minimize re-rupture, or non-op + accelerated rehab for lower complication risk? What influences your decision most?
