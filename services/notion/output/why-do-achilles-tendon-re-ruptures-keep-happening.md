## Content overview


**Why do Achilles Tendon Re-ruptures Keep Happening?**


Fresh data from a large case-control study highlights **WHO is most at RISK** and the factors driving re-rupture:

- 📊 **Incidence:** 0.94 per 100,00/year overall
- 👨 **Gender:** Men 7.4x more likely to re-rupture
- ⏳ **Timing:** Median 98.5 days post-rupture, but some occurred up to 50 years later
- 🧑‍🦱 **Younger** **Age:** <45 yrs = nearly double the risk
- 🩹 **Rehab:** Traditional cast immobilisation = 2.2x higher re-rupture risk

👉 Even **low-energy injuries** accounted for many cases.


With non-surgical protocols often defaulting to immobilisation, are we underestimating the long-term risk for younger, active patients?


source: [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35018477/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35018477/)
