## Content overview

📢 **Achilles News Flash: Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS) Now Outperforms Open Repair**

The choice of technique for surgical Achilles Tendon Rupture (ATR) repair is moving decisively toward **Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS)**, according to leading American and European orthopedic reviews.

MIS offers a safer path to tendon healing than the traditional open procedure.

### **The Key Findings: Safety & Efficiency**

- **Equal Re-Rupture Risk:** MIS techniques achieve a repair strength comparable to open surgery, resulting in **no significant difference** in the critical re-rupture rate.
- **Lower Complications:** MIS dramatically reduces the major risks associated with open surgery, including lower rates of:
  - **Infection**
  - **Wound Necrosis** (wound healing problems)
  - **Adhesions/Scarring**
- **Accelerated Start:** Less soft-tissue trauma allows for a quicker entry into the accelerated functional rehabilitation protocols (early weight-bearing and motion).

If you've had surgery, was your procedure Minimally Invasive or Open? What was your wound healing experience like?
