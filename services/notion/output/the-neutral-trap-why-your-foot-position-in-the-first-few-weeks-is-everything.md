## The "Neutral" Trap: Why your foot position in the first few weeks is everything.


When you first rupture your Achilles, the instinct is often to keep the foot at a comfortable 90-degree angle. In the medical world, we call this a "neutral" position. However, for a fresh tendon tear, neutral is actually a position of tension that pulls the healing tissue apart.


As the illustration shows, keeping the foot pointed down (plantarflexion) is the clinical "sweet spot." This position brings the torn tendon ends closer together, allowing them to bridge and knit back together effectively. If the ends are too far apart, the body fills the gap with weaker scar tissue, which can lead to a "long" tendon and reduced explosive power later on.


Early recovery isn't about testing your range of motion; it’s about **strict protection**. Every time the foot accidentally drifts toward neutral in those first few weeks, you risk stretching the repair before it has gained any structural integrity.


**The goal is simple: Keep the ends close. Keep them protected.**
