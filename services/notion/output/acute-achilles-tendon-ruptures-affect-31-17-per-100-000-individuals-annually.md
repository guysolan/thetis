🚨 **Did you know?** Acute Achilles tendon ruptures affect 31.17 per 100,000 individuals annually! This injury is particularly common in active, young adults but presents a significant debate on the best treatment approach: surgical vs non-surgical. 🤔


Here’s what the latest meta-analysis tells us:


🔹 **Re-rupture Rate:**


**Surgical group**: 3.63%


**Non-surgical group**: 10.1%


**Conclusion:** Surgical intervention significantly reduces the risk of re-rupture 📉


🔹 **Complications:**


**Surgical group**: Higher risk of **superficial wound infection** (6.6%) and **sural nerve injury** (4.2%)


**Non-surgical group**: Fewer complications overall, but with a **higher re-rupture rate** 😷


🔹 **Functional Outcomes:**


No significant difference in **range of motion**, **return to sport**, or **overall function** between the two treatments 🤷‍♂️


So, what’s the best approach for your patients or practice? Surgical techniques lower re-rupture risks, but the trade-off includes complications. Could minimally invasive surgical methods be the future? 🛠️
