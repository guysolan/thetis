Let’s be honest: Achilles Tendon Rupture (ATR) recovery isn't won in the clinic, it's won in the bedroom.


**If you treat ATRs remember that the greatest risk of re-rupture in the first 10 weeks is patient when patients remove their boot.**


One brilliant analogy is the game of **Snakes and Ladders.** Wrong wrong step sends you sliding down a snake and back to the start of recovery.


We know patients complain about sleeping in the heavy boot, but you need to stress that removing it for 'comfort' or hopping into the shower is where the vast majority of failures happen. That single, unguarded moment of ankle dorsiflexion can tear the fragile repair.


How do you explain to your patients the danger of re-rupture?
