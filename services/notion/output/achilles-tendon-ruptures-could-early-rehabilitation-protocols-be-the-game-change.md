### Achilles tendon ruptures: Could early rehabilitation protocols be the game-changer?


Did you know that only 2.7% of patients suffer re-ruptures after undergoing early functional rehabilitation following Achilles tendon surgery? 📊 That's a surprisingly low number, especially when you consider the severity of the injury.


Key Findings from Recent Research:


Re-rupture rate: Overall, 2.7% (ranging from 0.04% to 0.08%) for early weight-bearing and mobilization (WB + M) protocols.


Major complications: Only 2.6% experienced major complications, with WB + M showing the lowest rates (0.02%).


Minor complications: 11.8% of patients experienced minor complications, with WB + M again leading the way with just 0.04%.


Return to Work & Sports:


Return to work: Patients following WB + M protocols return to work significantly quicker, with some studies showing a time to return as short as 6.2 days.


Return to sports: Athletes following WB + M can return to sports in as little as 4 months, far quicker than more restrictive rehabilitation methods.


This study analyzed data from 20 randomized controlled trials and 1,007 patients. The takeaway? Early mobilization and weight-bearing protocols post-surgery lead to faster recovery times without compromising safety.
