## Content overview


**Percutaneous vs Open Repair in Athletes: What This Achilles Study Showed**



A study of 31 athletes with ruptured Achilles tendons compared **percutaneous surgery (PS)** and **standard open surgery (OS)** — evaluating functional recovery, muscle atrophy, strength, and return to sport at 15-month follow-up.


📈 **Return to Sport:** Athletes who underwent percutaneous repair returned in about **130 days**, versus **178 days** in open repair.


⚙️ **Muscle Atrophy:** PS patients had only ~13 mm atrophy vs ~24 mm for OS (statistically significant difference).


🦵 **Strength Deficit:** Both groups had a similar ~19% plantar-flexor strength loss.


✅ **Rerupture:** None reported in either group.


📊 **Functional Outcome:** Average AOFAS score was 94, with minimal pain (VAS 0.5). 77% returned to their pre-injury sport level.


**Question for clinicians:** Would you favour percutaneous repair in high-demand patients like athletes prioritizing speed and muscle preservation or stick to open repair for perceived long-term stability?
