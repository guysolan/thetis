## Content overview


**⚖️ MIS vs. Open ATR Repair: Rethinking the Sural Nerve Risk-Benefit Analysis**


A new umbrella review provides clarity on the long-debated trade-offs between surgical techniques for Acute Achilles Tendon Rupture (ATR).

- 📅 **Published:** Sept 2025 (Frontiers in Surgery - _Umbrella Review_)
- 🔪 **MIS Advantage:** Significantly reduced complication rates (wound infection, necrosis) and faster surgical time.
- ⚡ **Re-Rupture:** Both MIS and Open repair showed broadly **comparable** re-rupture rates, contrary to older concerns.
- ⚠️ **Key Risk:** MIS remains associated with a higher potential for **sural nerve injury** compared to Open repair.

While MIS offers less soft-tissue trauma, the risk of iatrogenic neurological complication is a critical factor for patient selection and surgeon technique.


💬 Given these balanced outcomes, at what point does a patient's risk profile (e.g., activity level, comorbidities) clearly push your decision toward an Open approach?
