Early Progressive Resistance After Achilles Rupture: Feasible and Promising

New:


**Reboot Recovery: Early Loading Can Protect Your Achilles Strength**



New: Jan 22, 2026 ↓


**Is early resistance training feasible after non-operative Achilles rupture?**


This feasibility study examined early progressive resistance exercises in patients treated non‑surgically for Achilles tendon ruptures.


Key take‑aways:

- 💪 Participants accepted and complied well with early resistance exercise programmes.
- 📉 While this study was not powered for full functional outcome differences, it suggests that **early progressive resistance is feasible** and may help reduce long‑term strength deficits.
- 🏋️ Rehabilitation emphasis shifts: not just immobilize, but initiate **structured resistance and loading earlier**, in selected cases.


🧑‍⚕️ **Clinical insight:** If you have access to a strong physio team and can monitor progression, introducing early resistance (after safe healing thresholds) might improve muscle strength recovery for non‑operative ruptures.


**Question:** Do you currently implement early resistance programmes for Achilles ruptures? What criteria do you use before starting them?
