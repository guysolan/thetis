## Achilles Rupture Recovery: The Sleep Challenge


Sleep disturbance is a common issue during the early rehabilitation phase after Achilles rupture.


Walking boots provide essential protection but are often heavy, rigid, and thermally uncomfortable, which can significantly affect sleep quality and patient adherence.


Many clinicians promote the use of **night splints** as a lighter alternative during sleep, maintaining plantarflexion while improving comfort compared with a full walking boot.


The key principle remains unchanged: **continuous protection of the healing tendon overnight to avoid elongation or re rupture.**


Do you advocate for the use of night splints during the boot phase, or require patients to keep the full walking boot on overnight?
