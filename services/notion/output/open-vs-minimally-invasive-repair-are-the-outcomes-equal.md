## Content overview


Open vs Minimally Invasive Repair — Are the Outcomes Equal?


Recent evidence comparing open surgical repair and minimally invasive surgery (MIS) for acute Achilles ruptures reveals interesting insights:

- 📌 **Re-rupture rates:** No significant difference between open and MIS techniques.
- 🩹 **Complication profile:** Open repair showed higher wound infection risk; MIS had higher sural nerve injury risk.
- 🔍 **Rehabilitation & function:** Both techniques delivered similar long-term functional outcomes when paired with structured rehab.

This suggests the surgeon’s skill set, patient factors (comorbidities, activity level), and rehab plan may matter **more** than the choice of open vs MIS alone.


**Question:**When you have the option, do you favour MIS for its complication profile, or open repair for established outcomes in your patients?
