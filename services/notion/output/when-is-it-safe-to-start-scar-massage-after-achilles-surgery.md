**Achilles Surgery Recovery: When to Start Scar Care**


## Facebook


**When Is It Safe to Start Scar Massage After Achilles Surgery?**


Around 2 weeks after Achilles surgery, most patients attend a wound check and suture removal appointment. This is a major milestone, but it doesn't mean it’s time to rush into intensive therapy. The most important rule for starting scar care is that the wound must be **fully closed** with no open areas or remaining scabs.


Once cleared by your specialist, gentle scar massage is an effective way to keep the tissue supple and improve mobility around the incision site. The goal isn't to be aggressive, but to be consistent. A simple approach involves using a moisturiser or oil to reduce friction and applying **light pressure only** in small circular movements.


Aim for 5 to 10 minutes of massage, 2 to 3 times per day. Remember, the specific product you use is less important than the consistency of the technique. Proper scar mobilisation helps prevent the skin from "tethering" to the underlying tendon, which is vital for a smooth return to movement.
