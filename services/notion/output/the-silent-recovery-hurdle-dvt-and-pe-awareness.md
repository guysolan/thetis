## The Silent Recovery Hurdle: DVT and PE Awareness


When you are immobilised in a recovery boot, your "calf pump"—the muscle action that helps push blood back up to your heart—is essentially switched off. This stagnation, combined with the body’s natural inflammatory response to an Achilles rupture, significantly increases the risk of a blood clot forming in the deep veins of your leg (**Deep Vein Thrombosis** or **DVT**).


While the focus is often on the tendon itself, you must stay vigilant about the systemic risks. A DVT becomes life-threatening if it breaks loose and travels to the lungs, causing a **Pulmonary Embolism** (PE). Understanding this "travel path" is not meant to cause alarm, but to ensure you are equipped with the knowledge to act fast.


**Watch for "new" calf pain that feels distinct from your rupture site, or swelling that seems isolated to just one leg.** If you suddenly experience breathlessness, sharp chest pain, or feel faint, these are red flags for a PE and require immediate emergency medical intervention. Early detection is the most effective way to keep your recovery journey safe and on track.
