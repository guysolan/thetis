### Patients Can’t Remember Everything They’re Told


In acute settings like A&E, patients are often in pain, stressed, and overloaded. Even when information is delivered clearly, much of it is not retained.


This often shows up at follow up as:

- Repeat questions
- Uncertainty around recovery steps
- Appointments spent re explaining rather than progressing care

This is not a communication failure. It reflects the reality of acute injury and stress.


Patients frequently return asking:


“What was said about this stage?”


“When was I meant to do that?”


Not because they weren’t told But because they couldn’t remember everything at the time. Structured recovery reminders can help.


When patients have access to clear, step by step guidance they can revisit:

- Information is reinforced outside the clinic
- Understanding improves over time
- Follow up appointments become more focused and productive

In practice, this supports:

- Better adherence to protocols
- Fewer repeat questions
- More efficient use of clinic time
- Better outcomes overall
