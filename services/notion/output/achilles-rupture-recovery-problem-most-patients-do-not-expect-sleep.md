### Achilles Rupture Recovery Problem Most Patients Do Not Expect: Sleep


Achilles Rupture Recovery Problem Most Patients Do Not Expect: Sleep


One of the most common complaints during Achilles rupture recovery is sleep disruption. Walking boots are often heavy, hot, and rigid, making it difficult to find a comfortable position while ensuring the tendon remains protected 24/7.


The key principle remains the same: protection overnight is essential. Without the boot or a splint, your foot can inadvertently move into a neutral or "toes-up" position, which risks tendon elongation or even re-rupture of the healing tissue.


A practical option some clinicians recommend is a night splint. These are typically lighter and more breathable than a full walking boot while still maintaining the required "toes-down" (plantarflexed) position. However, this is always subject to your specific surgeon's or physio's protocol.
