Is your athlete ready to return to sports after an Achilles tendon rupture? 🤔


Surprisingly, the return-to-sport (RTS) rates after Achilles tendon ruptures (ATR) range from 61% to 100%. But it all depends on the athlete's level and sport! 🏀⚽


Key Stats


Rerupture rates: Surgical treatment can significantly lower rerupture risks, but it's important to monitor post-op progress carefully.


Plantar flexion strength: Expect 10-30% deficits up to a year post-surgery.


Heel-rise endurance: Deficits of 20-30% persist for over 12 months after ATR.


Time to running: Athletes typically begin running 12-16 weeks after surgery. The exact time can be decided based on the results of their strength tests and mechanics.


The RTS Phase (24+ weeks) focuses on:


Restoring strength (≥90% of pre-injury levels)


Monitoring loading through sport-specific drills


Implementing functional training techniques


⚡ Are your athletes ready for the sport?


It's crucial to assess factors like isometric plantar flexion strength (1.5–2x BW), single-leg heel raises, and plyometric readiness before transitioning to more intense movements.


Source: Rehabilitation and Return to Sports after Achilles Tendon Repair - [https://doi.org/10.22203/eCM.v043a12](https://doi.org/10.22203/eCM.v043a12)
