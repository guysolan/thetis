## Beyond the Boot: Managing Your Clot Risk


Immobilisation after Achilles rupture increases clot risk.


Depending on individual risk and local protocol, patients may receive:

- **Injections (e.g. enoxaparin)** – established but require daily self injection
- **Oral tablets (e.g. rivaroxaban or apixaban)** – convenient but not suitable for everyone

Education is just as important as medication.


**DVT signs:** new calf pain, one leg swelling, warmth, tightness.


**PE signs (emergency):** sudden breathlessness, chest pain, fainting.


Clot prevention starts with awareness.
