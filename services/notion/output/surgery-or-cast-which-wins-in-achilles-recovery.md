## Content overview


Surgery or Cast: Which Wins in Achilles Recovery? ⚡


A major randomized controlled trial (RCT) published in the New England Journal of Medicine (Apr 14, 2022) compared non-operative care, open repair surgery, and minimally invasive surgery (MIS) in 526 patients with acute Achilles tendon ruptures.


Here’s what they found:


526 patients (18–60 yrs) randomized across 14 hospitals in Norway


⚡ Function (ATRS at 12 months):
– Non-operative: –17.0 pts
– Open surgery: –16.0 pts
– MIS: –14.7 pts
No significant difference in recovery (p = 0.57)


📉 Re-rupture rates at 12 months:
– Non-operative: 6.2%
– Surgery (open/MIS): 0.6%
Surgery dramatically lowers re-rupture risk


⚠️ Nerve injuries:
– MIS: 5.2%
– Open: 2.8%
– Non-operative: 0.6%
Surgery carries higher complication risk, especially MIS


This trial highlights the trade-offs: surgery may reduce re-rupture, but comes with higher risk of nerve complications.

 In your practice, do you prioritize **lower re-rupture rates** with surgery, or prefer the **safety of non-operative care**?
