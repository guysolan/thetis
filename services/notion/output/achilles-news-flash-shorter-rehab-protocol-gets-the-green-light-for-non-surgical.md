Achilles News Flash: Shorter Rehab Protocol Gets the Green Light for Non-Surgical ATR 👟



Great news for patients managing an Acute Achilles Tendon Rupture (**ATR**) non-operatively! 🥳


A recent study investigated whether the duration of a functional weight-bearing orthosis impacts recovery, comparing an **8-week** dynamic rehabilitation protocol to a longer **11-week** one.



### **The Key Finding: No Detriment in Shorter Course**

- **Equal Functional Outcomes:** The study found **no significant difference** in long-term functional results (as measured by the Achilles Tendon Rupture Score - ATRS) between the 8-week and 11-week groups. Both groups achieved comparable average ATRS scores (approx. 76).
- **Complication Rates:** The shorter 8-week regime did not lead to a significant increase in re-ruptures or other major complications.
- **The Trend:** This reinforces the modern shift towards **accelerated functional rehabilitation** protocols, which prioritize early range of motion and weight-bearing to optimize recovery, regardless of the overall time spent in the boot.
