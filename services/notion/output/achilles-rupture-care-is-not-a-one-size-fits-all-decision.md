## Content overview


**Achilles rupture care is not a one size fits all decision.**



The choice between surgical and non surgical treatment should be guided by **patient factors**, not headlines.


Most clinicians recognise that:

- Surgical repair may reduce re rupture risk and speed return to sport, but with higher complication risk
- Modern non surgical pathways with early functional rehab can achieve comparable long term outcomes
- Orthopaedic devices play a critical supportive role in protection, comfort, and rehab compliance

Yet in practice, decisions are rarely straightforward.


Age, activity level, comorbidities, and a patient’s ability to commit to rehabilitation often matter more than protocols alone.


**Where do you personally draw the line?**


For example:

- High demand athlete but poor rehab compliance
- Lower demand patient with strong rehab support

Which factor tends to carry the most weight in your decision making?
