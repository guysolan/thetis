## Notes


Achilles tendon injuries are skyrocketing. Here’s why it matters to YOU! 🚨


The Achilles tendon, while the largest and strongest tendon in the body, is surprisingly vulnerable to injury.


🔸 Key Mechanisms of Injury


·      High-Impact Load: Sudden dorsiflexion or violent ankle movements, especially in sports like football, tennis, and badminton, are common causes.


·      Acceleration-Deceleration: Up to 90% of sports-related Achilles tendon ruptures occur during rapid changes in speed. 🏃‍♂️💨


·      Degenerative Tendon Changes: Over time, tendons can lose their tensile strength, making them more prone to rupture.


🔸 Other Contributing Factors


·      Poor technique, unconditioned muscles, and inadequate footwear can amplify the risk.


·      Health conditions like diabetes, hyperthyroidism, and high serum lipid levels can also increase the risk of these injuries.


With the right understanding, we can better prevent and treat Achilles tendon ruptures. This ultimately reduces recovery time and improves return-to-play outcomes. 🏅
