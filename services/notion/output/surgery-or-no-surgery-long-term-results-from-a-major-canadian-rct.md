## Content overview


**Surgery or No Surgery? Long-Term Results From a Major Canadian RCT**


One of the most influential Achilles rupture trials came from **Canada**, comparing operative vs non-operative treatment using an accelerated functional rehab protocol.


**Here’s what the study revealed 👇**
📉 **Re-rupture rates were similar** when early functional rehabilitation was used in both groups.
🏃‍♂️ **Return-to-sport timelines showed no major differences**, suggesting rehab quality may matter more than the surgical decision in many cases.
🩺 **Surgical patients had more complications**, mainly wound issues — a key factor when counseling active patients.



💡 **Takeaway:** With the right functional rehabilitation, non-operative treatment can perform just as well as surgery for many patients — shifting the focus toward rehab access and patient selection.



**Question:**


Do you think rehab quality is now the biggest factor in determining outcomes after ATR?
