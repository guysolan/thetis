## Content overview


**Open vs MIS Repair: What’s the Real Advantage?**


A recent meta-analysis comparing surgical techniques for **acute Achilles tendon rupture** found that both **open repair** and **minimally invasive surgery (MIS)** significantly reduce **re-rupture rates** compared to non-operative treatment.


📊 The surgical group showed re-rupture rates as low as **~2% (95% CI 1–3%)** versus **~12%** for non-surgical approaches.


⚠️ However, complication risks remain higher in surgical options — for example, **sural nerve injury rates up to 4%** in percutaneous/MIS cases compared with **~1%** in open repair. Meanwhile, **functional outcomes** such as return to sport, ankle range of motion, and patient-reported ATRS were often comparable between techniques.


💡 For clinicians, this suggests that when repair is indicated, **MIS may offer the best balance** of low re-rupture and manageable complications — assuming appropriate **surgeon experience** and **patient selection**.


💬 Question: In your practice, do you favour **MIS when feasible**, or stick with **open repair** for consistency and familiarity?
