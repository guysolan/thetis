## 🏥 Achilles Rupture: Surgery vs. Non-Operative Treatment


One of the biggest early decisions in Achilles rupture care is whether to operate. According to the comprehensive systematic review and meta-analysis by **Ochen et al. (2019)**, both surgical and non-surgical approaches can achieve excellent functional outcomes for many patients.


The choice often comes down to the **risk profile** and **patient context**, rather than one being "better" than the other.


### 🔍 Clarifying the Evidence: Ochen et al. vs. UKSTAR


It’s important to clarify a common point of confusion in clinical discussions regarding the evidence base:

1. **The Comparison (Ochen et al.,** _**BMJ**_ **2019):** This study directly compared **Operative vs. Non-operative** treatment. It found that surgery reduces the risk of re-rupture by approximately **7%** compared to non-operative treatment, but carries a higher risk of other complications (such as infection).
2. **The Protocol (UKSTAR Trial):** Crucially, the **UKSTAR trial** did _not_ compare surgery to non-surgery. Instead, it examined two different **non-operative** pathways:
    - Traditional plaster cast immobilisation
    - Early weight-bearing in a functional brace

**UKSTAR’s conclusion?** Early weight-bearing in a functional brace is a safe, cost-effective alternative to casting with no difference in functional outcomes (ATRS) or re-rupture rates at 9 months.


### ⚖️ The Takeaway for Shared Decision-Making


When we discuss "Non-Op" today, we aren't talking about 8 weeks in a heavy cast. We are talking about **functional rehabilitation**.


By using **Ochen et al.** to weigh the surgical risks and **UKSTAR** to optimize the non-surgical pathway, we can provide patients with a much clearer picture of their recovery journey.
