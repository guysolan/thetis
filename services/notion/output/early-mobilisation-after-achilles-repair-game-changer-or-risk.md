## Content overview


Early Mobilisation After Achilles Repair — Game Changer or Risk?


Recovery protocols following **Achilles tendon repair** are evolving. 🦵 A **2024 meta-analysis** assessed rehabilitation timelines and found that **accelerated rehabilitation** (early weight bearing and active motion) may shorten recovery **without increasing re-rupture risk**.


✅ Patients in the early mobilisation groups showed **comparable re-rupture rates** and **fewer stiffness-related complications** than those under prolonged immobilisation.


📈 A systematic review also found that about **80% of patients** returned to sport — though outcomes varied depending on rehab protocol and RTP definitions.


🧠 Clinical takeaway: rehabilitation should not wait for “perfect” healing alone — **early, controlled loading** and **functional progression** may be key to better outcomes.


In your protocol, how soon do you initiate **active motion or weight-bearing** after Achilles repair — and what factors influence your decision?
