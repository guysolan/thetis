## Content overview


**Early Controlled Motion After Achilles Repair — Safe or Risky?**


An RCT and meta-analysis from Europe investigated whether early controlled motion following Achilles rupture (operative or non-operative) improves outcomes without sacrificing safety.


Highlights:

- 📈 Patients who began motion earlier (weeks 3–8) showed **improved ankle mobility** and **reduced stiffness**.
- 📉 There was **no significant increase in re-rupture rate** in early motion cohorts compared to traditional immobilization.
- 🧩 However, the quality of evidence remains moderate and patient selection is critical.

These findings suggest that in centers with structured rehab and monitoring, **early controlled motion** may enhance recovery — but it must be paired with careful protocol adherence.


**Question:** When do you begin controlled motion after Achilles repair in your protocol — and how do you monitor for risk of elongation or re-injury?
