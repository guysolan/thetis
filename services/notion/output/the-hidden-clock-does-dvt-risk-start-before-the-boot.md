**The Hidden Clock: Does DVT Risk Start Before the Boot?**


In a study of patients with acute Achilles tendon ruptures, the findings were startling: **42% of patients had a distal vein thrombosis detected via ultrasound before any immobilisation or casting had even taken place**. Most of these clots were found in the muscular veins of the calf—the gastrocnemius or soleus—rather than the deep primary veins. Interestingly, the majority of these individuals lacked traditional DVT risk factors.


While this doesn't prove the rupture itself causes the clot, it is the first publication to link muscular vein thrombosis to the **pre-immobilisation phase**. This suggests that the injury and the immediate drop in movement may be enough to trigger a risk. For you, this means staying vigilant for symptoms like unusual swelling or calf pain from **"Day Zero,"** rather than waiting until you are fitted for a boot.


Did your doctor check you for blood clots or start prevention on the same day your Achilles rupture happened? 🦶
