## Content overview


Is Early Controlled Motion Safe for Non-Operative Achilles Ruptures?



A 2021 systematic review of 18 studies (1,068 patients) evaluated non-operative Achilles ruptures managed with **early weight-bearing and controlled motion** versus traditional immobilization

✅ **Findings:** Early WB & controlled motion did **not** increase re-rupture risk (overall re-rupture 7.3%), and patients returned to work earlier (~10.3 weeks on average)


📈 Functional outcomes — using the Achilles Tendon Total Rupture Score (ATRS) — were comparable or slightly better in early WB groups than late WB groups.



These findings suggest that, for selected patients, non-operative management with early functional rehab can be a **safe, effective alternative** — challenging the assumption that conservative care must always mean prolonged immobilization.



**Question:** In your practice — when choosing non-operative management — do you consider early weight-bearing + controlled motion? What factors (age, compliance, activity level) influence that choice?
