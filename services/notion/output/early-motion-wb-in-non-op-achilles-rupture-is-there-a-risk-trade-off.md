## Content overview


**Early Motion & WB in Non-Op Achilles Rupture — Is There a Risk Trade-Off?**

A recent meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials (nine RCTs, 1,046 patients) examined **early controlled motion and weight-bearing** versus immobilization in conservatively managed Achilles tendon ruptures.

Here’s what they reported:


🔁 **Re-rupture risk:** No statistically significant difference between early WB and late WB (RR = 0.75).


📈 **Functional outcomes:** ATRS scores were nearly identical between both groups.


✅ **Work and sport return:** Time to return and adverse events did not differ strongly.


Another RCT (Denmark) supports this: patients who started full WB immediately had similar functional scores at 1 year, with no meaningful increase in re-rupture.


These data support a shift toward **functional non-operative care**, especially in settings where patient compliance is high and structured rehab is possible.


**Question:** For non-operative ruptures, do you lean toward early WB? If so, how do you counsel patients about risks and benefits
