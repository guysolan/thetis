## Content overview


### **Operative vs Non-Operative Achilles Rupture Treatment: What Really Changes?**


A 2019 meta-analysis published in the _BMJ_ evaluated multiple randomized trials comparing surgical repair to non-operative management in acute Achilles Tendon ruptures.


Key findings:

- 🔹 **Re-rupture rate** significantly lower with surgery vs non-operative treatment.
- ⚠️ **Complication rate** higher in the surgical group (including wound issues and nerve injury).
- 📊 **Long-term functional outcomes** (return to sport, patient-reported scores) were largely comparable between both treatment paths.

For clinicians, the takeaway is clear: while surgery may reduce re-rupture risk, the decision should also factor patient comorbidities, functional goals, rehab access, and complication risk.


**Question:**


In your practice, how do you balance the lower risk of re-rupture with the higher complication risk when deciding between operative and non-operative care?
