Achilles Boot Mistake Most Patients Make: Removing Wedges Too Fast


Early Achilles rupture rehabilitation relies on a carefully controlled reduction of plantarflexion. Most protocols begin with your foot held in a maximally pointed-down position to keep the torn ends of the tendon close together, then gradually move toward neutral as the tissue matures.


Reducing the angle of your foot too quickly is one of the most common recovery mistakes. If you remove wedges before the tendon has gained sufficient strength, you risk tendon elongation—healing "long"—which can lead to a permanent loss of power in your calf. Following the progression allows the healing tissue to adapt safely to increasing tension.


Typical progression looks like this:


Weeks 0 to 5: Maximum plantarflexion to protect the initial "knit".


Week 5 onwards: Gradual wedge removal or hinge adjustment to start adding light tension.


Weeks 8 to 10: The foot approaches a neutral 90-degree angle.


After ~10 weeks: The transition out of the boot into standard footwear begins.
