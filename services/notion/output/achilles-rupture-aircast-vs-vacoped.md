**Achilles Rupture: Aircast vs. VACOped**


Walking boots are a foundational part of early Achilles rupture management, yet the choice between models often causes unnecessary stress for patients. The two most common contenders are the **Aircast and the VACOped**. While they look different, their primary mission is identical: protecting the tendon as it heals.


The Aircast is a lightweight, widely available option that uses removable heel wedges to gradually lower your foot from a pointed position toward neutral. In contrast, the VACOped uses an adjustable hinge mechanism and is often considered a more dynamic choice, though it is typically heavier and more expensive.


In practice, high-quality recovery depends far more on **protocol adherence and rehabilitation progression** than the specific brand of boot on your leg. Most patients will successfully recover using whichever boot their hospital provides. The "magic" isn't in the plastic; it’s in the consistent protection of the tendon length during those first crucial weeks.


If you were given a choice, which boot did you find more comfortable for daily wear during your recovery? 🦶
