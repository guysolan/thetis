## Your Achilles patient says they’re ‘back’ but are they really performing?


Return-to-sport doesn’t always mean return-to-performance. A large U.S. military study shows why expectations matter. 🏃‍♂️


**Key clinical takeaways:**


🔹 Runners were **~41 seconds slower** on a 1.5-mile run after Achilles repair


🔹 **69% still maintained or improved** their fitness category


🔹 Running performance **continued to improve beyond 1 year** post-op


🔹 True test failures were **rare (≤1.5%)** in an active population


**What this means in practice:**


✅ Early “return” ≠ full performance recovery


✅ Running may lag behind cycling and low-impact cardio


✅ Long-term conditioning matters as much as surgical success


✅ Patients need **performance-based counseling**, not just clearance dates


Setting realistic timelines helps prevent frustration, non-compliance, and premature overload.


**Clinical question:** Do you track _objective performance metrics_ after Achilles repair — or rely mainly on return-to-activity milestones?
