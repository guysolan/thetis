## Content overview


**🏃‍♂️ Rehabilitation Revolution: Full Weight-Bearing Earlier is the New Normal 💯**



The non-operative management of Acute Achilles Tendon Rupture (**ATR**) has seen a dramatic, evidence-based shift in clinical practice. The days of prolonged non-weight-bearing (**NWB**) are over🚫


**The Key Details: Accelerated Loading** ⚡


📅 **Source:** Multiple Western Reviews & RCTs (2020-2024)


**New Protocol:** Early functional rehab, often starting **partial to full weight-bearing (FWB) within weeks 2-4** in a controlled boot. 👟


**Outcome:** Early Weight-Bearing (**EWB**) protocols show **equivalent or lower re-rupture rates** while significantly reducing stiffness and accelerating the return to daily function. ✅



**Clinical Insight** 💡


The consensus is clear: controlled, early loading promotes a better biological healing response without compromise. Maximize weight-bearing as early as patient tolerance and tendon stability permit! 💪



**Question:** What is your clinic's current timeline for progressing non-operative ATR patients to full weight-bearing? Is the patient compliance better? 🤔
Authors: Mr Josh Ferguson, APAM, Sports and Exercise Physiotherapist, and Dr Alicia James PhD, APodA, Credentialed Paediatric Podiatrist and Head of Podiatry, Peninsula Health.
