## Does Limb Dominance Affect Achilles Rupture Recovery?


Most Achilles rupture discussions focus on _how_ we repair the tendon.


This study asks a different question: **does** _**which**_ **leg ruptures matter?** 🦶



A 2-year follow-up study of surgically repaired acute Achilles tendon ruptures found:


⚖️ **Higher ATRS scores** when the rupture occurred in the **dominant limb**


📏 **Greater calf atrophy** on the operated side, regardless of dominance


🔍 **Increased tendon thickness and length** on ultrasound, without functional loss


🏃 **85.7% return-to-sport rate** at a mean of **5.8 months**, supported by early rehab



Despite structural changes on imaging, **functional outcomes remained strong**, especially in dominant limbs.



This suggests neuromuscular adaptation, habitual loading, and limb-specific motor control may influence recovery more than we assume.



**Clinical question:** Do you adjust rehab expectations or timelines based on limb dominance in active patients?
