## Content overview


Thromboembolic risk is usually linked to casting or surgery.



**New:** Could thrombosis begin before immobilisation in Achilles rupture?



This prospective study asked a different question — can thrombosis already be present at first presentation? 🩸


In 21 patients presenting with acute Achilles tendon rupture:


🩺 **42% had a distal vein thrombosis** detected on Doppler ultrasound **before immobilization**


🦵 Most thrombi were located in **muscular veins** gastrocnemius or soleus


⚠️ Most patients **lacked classic DVT risk factors**


🫁 **No pulmonary embolism** occurred in this cohort


This is the **first publication** to associate muscular vein thrombosis with the **pre immobilization phase** of Achilles rupture.


The authors are clear — this does not prove causation, but raises important questions about early screening and risk stratification.


**Clinical question:** Should DVT risk assessment start earlier in Achilles rupture care pathways?


**Authors:** Gaston Slullitel, Valeria Lopez, Juan Pablo Calvi, Gaitan Laura, Mark Mayerson
