Operative vs Non-Operative Achilles Rupture: What the Evidence Show


You’ll probably see 1000 Achilles rupture thousand patients in your career. Would you prefer 23 re-ruptures and 49 complications or 39 re-ruptures and 16 complications?


A tricky question, but one you’re making based on your decision to operate or not operate on ATRs…

A systematic review and meta-analysis published in _BMJ_ compared operative and non-operative treatment of acute Achilles tendon ruptures.



Key findings:

- 🔹 **Re-rupture rate** was lower after surgery: 2.3% vs 3.9% for non-operative care (risk ratio 0.43).
- ⚠️ **Complication rate** was higher with surgery: 4.9% vs 1.6%, mostly due to infections.
- 📊 **Functional outcomes** were similar between both groups, especially with accelerated rehabilitation and early range of motion.

**Question:** What do you choose? Risk of re-rupture or infection?
