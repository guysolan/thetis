## **Are ‘Equivalent’ Re-Rupture Rates in Achilles Care More Fragile Than We Think?**


We often hear that **surgery and early functional rehab have similar re rupture rates** after Achilles rupture. But how strong is that claim, statistically?


A **2023 systematic review of randomized trials** looked at this question using a tool called the **Reverse Fragility Index RFI** 📊


Here’s what they found 👇


🔁 **Average re rupture rates across trials**

- Surgery: **4 percent**
- Early functional rehab: **10 percent**

📉 **Reverse Fragility Index = 3**


This means that changing the outcome of **just 3 patients** in a typical trial would be enough to change the conclusion.


⚠️ **77.8% of trials** had more patients lost to follow-up than their RFI — meaning conclusions may be unstable.


👉 Takeaway:


Claims of “no difference” between surgery and early rehab may rest on **very fragile statistical ground**.


Clinical decisions should consider not only point estimates, but also how **robust the evidence truly is**.


Question: Do you factor study fragility or loss-to-follow-up when interpreting Achilles rupture trials?
