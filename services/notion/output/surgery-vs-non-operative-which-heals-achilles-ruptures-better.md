**Would you prefer an extra 12% risk of complications or an extra 6% risk of re-rupture?**


A landmark study in _The Journal of Bone and Joint Surgery_ (2010) compared surgical vs. non-operative treatment for acute Achilles tendon ruptures in active patients. Surgery reduced re-ruptures but increased complication risks.


Here’s what they found 👇


🔁 **Re-rupture Rates:**


– Surgery: 3.16%


– Non-op: 9.62%


⚠️ **Complications:**


– Surgery: 18.98%


– Non-op: 6.22%



Trade-off: Surgery lowers re-rupture risk but carries higher infection and nerve injury risk. Treatment choice should balance **risk vs. benefit** based on patient factors and activity level.


Given the trade-offs, how do you approach treatment decisions for Achilles tendon ruptures in your practice?
