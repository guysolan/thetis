# **Surgery vs. Non-Surgical: The Big Decision**


One of the biggest early decisions in Achilles rupture care is whether to operate. The reassuring news is that high-quality evidence suggests both approaches work well for most patients. The UKSTAR trial, the largest randomised study in this area, confirmed that functional outcomes at two years are remarkably similar.


The choice isn't about which method "works" and which doesn't; it is about your specific **risk profile and patient-specific context**. Surgery carries a 2.3% re-rupture rate but a 2.8% risk of infection. Non-surgical treatment avoids infection entirely but has a slightly higher re-rupture risk at 3.9%.


Ultimately, your activity level, wound risk, and ability to comply with rehabilitation matter most. Framing this decision clearly reduces anxiety and allows for **better shared decision-making** between you and your specialist.
