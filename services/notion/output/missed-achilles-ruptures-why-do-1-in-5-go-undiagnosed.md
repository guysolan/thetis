## Content overview


Missed Achilles Ruptures: Why Do 1 in 5 Go Undiagnosed?


Up to 20% of Achilles ruptures are initially misdiagnosed — often mistaken for ankle sprains. This delay in diagnosis can lead to poor outcomes, delayed rehab, and a higher risk of long-term dysfunction.


A systematic review and meta-analysis looked at the accuracy of ultrasound in detecting Achilles ruptures, using surgery as the reference standard.


Here’s what they found:


🔍 Sensitivity: 94.8% (excellent at detecting ruptures)
✅ Specificity: 98.7% (rarely produces false positives)
📈 Positive likelihood ratio: 74.0
📉 Negative likelihood ratio: 0.05


⚠️ Yet despite these numbers, many ruptures are still missed clinically — often dismissed as minor ankle sprains.


The takeaway? Early imaging and careful physical exam are key to avoiding missed diagnoses. A negative ultrasound can effectively rule out rupture, while clinical suspicion should always remain high in acute ankle injuries.
