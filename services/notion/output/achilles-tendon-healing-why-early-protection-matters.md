### Achilles Tendon Healing: Why Early Protection Matters


Tendon healing after Achilles rupture follows three biological phases.


Weeks 1–2: Inflammatory phase
Weeks 3–6: Proliferative phase — new collagen bridges the gap
Weeks 6–12+: Remodelling phase — tissue reorganises and strengthens


During the proliferative stage, newly formed collagen is still fragile.
Excessive early loading or dorsiflexion can risk tendon elongation.


This is why strict adherence to immobilisation protocols and gradual progression remains critical in early rehabilitation.


For orthopaedic surgeons managing Achilles rupture protocols, how do you balance early mobilisation with tendon protection during the proliferative healing phase?
