## Content overview


Calf weakness after Achilles rupture is common and often persistent.



This randomized controlled trial examined whether **blood flow restriction BFR therapy** could reduce early strength loss with low load rehab 🦵


In **43 patients** undergoing surgical Achilles tendon repair:


⚙️ **Absolute plantarflexion strength and power** were significantly higher in the BFR group at **3 months**


📊 Improvements were seen at **both testing speeds** 60° and 120° per second


⚖️ **No significant improvement** was found in strength relative to the non operative leg


📏 **Calf circumference** showed a positive but **non significant** association with BFR p 0.59


No significant differences were seen in dorsiflexion or knee strength.


Importantly, BFR improved **absolute strength**, but **did not normalize limb symmetry**, reinforcing its role as an adjunct rather than a replacement for progressive loading.


**Clinical question:** Where does BFR fit in your post operative Achilles rehab protocol?


**Authors:** Oliver B Hansen, Andrea Papson, Stephanie K Eble, Mark C Drakos
