## Content overview


**Would You Rather Risk a Re-Rupture or a Wound Infection?**


A comprehensive 2025 systematic review and meta-analysis encompassing multiple high-quality trials found the following in acute Achilles tendon ruptures:

- 🔄 **Re-rupture rate:** Significantly lower with surgical repair compared to non-operative care.
- ⚠️ **Complications:** Surgical group had higher incidence of wound infections, sural nerve injury, and stiffness.
- 📊 **Functional outcomes:** Long-term scores (return to sport, strength, satisfaction) showed no significant difference between treatment paths.

These findings reinforce that while surgery reduces re-rupture risk, the trade-off includes higher complication risk — and strong rehabilitation protocols remain crucial for both pathways.


**Question:**In your practice, how do you weigh **lower chance of re-rupture** against **higher complication risk** when choosing treatment strategy?
