## Content overview


Is Non-Operative Achilles Treatment Truly ‘Equivalent’ — Or Just Underpowered?



Non-operative management with early functional rehabilitation is often promoted as having **equal re-rupture risk** compared to surgery.


A 2022 **critical analysis of meta-analyses** challenges that assumption.


Key findings: 👇


📉 Only **1 of 6 pooled comparisons** showed a statistically significant reduction in re-rupture favoring surgery.


⚖️ However, the data **could not exclude** a clinically meaningful benefit for surgery:

- Relative risk as low as **0.28**
- Absolute risk difference up to **6.4% higher re-rupture** with non-operative care

Complication outcomes showed **large variability and imprecision**, limiting strong conclusions.


👉 The authors conclude that the growing shift toward non-operative treatment is **not clearly supported** by current evidence.


Question: When counseling patients, how do you explain uncertainty around re-rupture risk with early functional rehab?
