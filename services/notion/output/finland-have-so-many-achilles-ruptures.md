## Our Thoughts


**📊 STRIKING DIFFERENCE: Why are Achilles rupture rates 4-8x higher in Finland compared to the US and UK?**


We at Thetis Medical found this fascinating study on Achilles tendon ruptures in Finland and couldn't help but notice something remarkable when comparing international data:

- Finland reports significantly higher rates of Achilles ruptures compared to other countries
- US data shows only 2.5 ruptures per 100,000 people (2012-2016)
- UK reports 8 ruptures per 100,000 people (2015-2021)
- Finland's numbers are substantially higher over a 20-year period

What could explain this dramatic difference? Is it:

- Different diagnostic criteria?
- Better detection methods in Finland?
- Genetic predisposition in Finnish population?
- Environmental factors cold climate?
- Specific Finnish activities (too much Ice Hockey and Floor Ball)!?
- Reporting differences between healthcare systems?

**Calling all clinicians and researchers:** What's your hypothesis for why Finland sees so many more Achilles ruptures than other countries? Could this knowledge help improve detection and treatment worldwide? 🤔
