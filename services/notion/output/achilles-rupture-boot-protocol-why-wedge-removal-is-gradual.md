**Achilles Rupture Boot Protocol: Why Wedge Removal Is Gradual**


Early Achilles rupture rehabilitation relies on controlled reduction of plantarflexion.


Most protocols begin with the foot held in a maximally plantarflexed position, then gradually move toward neutral as the tendon heals.


Typical progression:

- Weeks 0 to 5: Maximum plantarflexion
- Week 5 onwards: Gradual wedge removal or hinge adjustment
- Weeks 8 to 10: Foot approaches neutral
- After ~10 weeks: Transition out of the boot

The key principle is gradual load adaptation.


Reducing the angle too quickly risks tendon elongation or re rupture.
Following the protocol allows the healing tissue to adapt safely.
