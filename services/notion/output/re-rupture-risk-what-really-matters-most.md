## Content overview


Re-rupture remains one of the most concerning complications following Achilles tendon rupture — but how much do surgery type and rehab strategy truly affect it?


A 2024 meta-analysis examining **over 15,000 cases** compared surgical and conservative management. Here’s what the data revealed 👇


🔁 Re-rupture rates:


– Surgical: **2.5–5%**


– Non-surgical: **4–12%**


⚠️ Complication rates:


– Surgical: **Up to 15%** (mostly wound-related)


– Non-surgical: **4–6%**


💡Takeaway: Surgery may reduce re-rupture risk, but only when paired with structured, gradual rehabilitation and protective immobilization during early recovery.


As more studies emphasize patient-specific risk factors — age, BMI, tendon gap size, and compliance — the best approach may not be surgical _or_ conservative, but strategic.
