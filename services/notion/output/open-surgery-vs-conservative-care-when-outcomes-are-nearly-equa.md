## Content overview


Open Surgery vs Conservative Care: When Outcomes Are Nearly Equal


A non-inferiority trial including 422 patients with acute Achilles ruptures compared surgical repair vs conservative treatment at 12 months follow-up.



🔹 **Functional outcomes** — heel-rise height, power, repetitions — showed **no significant differences** between both groups.
🔹 The patient-reported ATRS was similarly equivalent, suggesting strength, mobility, and daily function may recover equally, regardless of surgery.


The takeaway: surgical repair reduces re-rupture risk, but **non-operative care remains a valid option** — especially when paired with structured rehab and patient compliance.


**Question:** Given these findings — in patients without high demand (non-athletes) — would you consider conservative care as first-line? Why or why not?
