Surgeons, achieving true equinus is non-negotiable for optimal Achilles Tendon Rupture (ATR) healing.


**The Concern:** Data suggests the **Aircast** (∼28∘ plantarflexion) may achieve equinus through **midfoot compensation**, while the **VACOped** (∼48∘) offers a truer tibiotalar angle (TTA).


**The Practical Reality:** The **Aircast (or other walkers) remains a practical solution** given its ∼$225 total setup cost vs. ∼$415 for VACOped. **If choosing non VACOped, be rigorous** in ensuring your patient's foot position is optimal _with the wedges_ at the initial stage. **Correct wedge placement and progression can mitigate** the risk of suboptimal positioning.
