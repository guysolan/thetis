Create a single high-resolution PATIENT-EDUCATION comparison graphic that answers the question: "How do I know if my walking boot fits correctly?"

OVERALL LAYOUT
- Clean, professional medical-education poster on a pure WHITE background.
- A bold dark title strip across the very top with the question: "How do I know if my walking boot fits correctly?"
- Below the title, a neat 2x2 GRID of four equally sized, evenly spaced photographic panels (same dimensions, same margins, same camera distance and angle for every panel so they look like a consistent set).
- Each panel has a thin rounded border: GREEN border for correct examples, RED border for the incorrect example.
- Every panel shows the SAME device from the base photo (teal / seafoam-green moulded splint shell, black Velcro straps, small white "Thetis Medical" label) on a person's lower leg and foot resting on white bedsheets, photorealistic, soft natural lighting matching the base photo.

THE FOUR PANELS
Three panels are GOOD FIT (correct). One panel is BAD FIT (incorrect). Make the BAD FIT panel OBVIOUSLY different at a glance — do not make the four images nearly identical. Vary the foot position and splint placement clearly.

GOOD FIT panel A (green):
- Heel pushed fully back, seated firmly at the back of the splint.
- Foot perfectly centered and straight inside the shell.
- Green check mark icon. Short green callout label: "Heel seated fully back".

GOOD FIT panel B (green):
- Both black straps evenly tightened, lying flat and parallel, snug and secure.
- Green check mark icon. Short green callout label: "Straps even & secure".

GOOD FIT panel C (green):
- Toes resting naturally near the front opening with a small gap, not cramped or jammed.
- Foot aligned with the splint, no sideways tilt.
- Green check mark icon. Short green callout label: "Toes natural, foot aligned".

BAD FIT panel (red):
- Make the problems unmistakable: the heel is clearly lifted / shifted FORWARD with a visible gap behind the heel at the back of the splint; the foot has slid forward so the toes are jammed against the front; the splint sits crooked / off-center; one black strap is loose and dangling, the other is twisted and uneven.
- Red circles around the heel gap and the loose strap, plus red arrows showing the foot sliding forward and the heel lifting.
- Short red callout labels: "Heel not seated", "Foot sliding forward", "Loose, uneven straps".

LABELS & INDICATORS
- Put a clear pill-shaped badge on each panel: GREEN badge reading "GOOD FIT" on the three correct panels, RED badge reading "BAD FIT" on the incorrect panel.
- Use GREEN (forest green, approx #2F5D45) for ALL correct indicators (checks, borders, badges, callouts).
- Use RED for ALL incorrect indicators (circles, arrows, borders, badge, callouts).
- Keep all text short, crisp, correctly spelled, sans-serif, highly legible.

STYLE
- Realistic photography (not illustration), patient-friendly, calm and clinical.
- Clean modern layout, consistent panel size and spacing, generous white space.
- High resolution, square overall composition.
- No watermarks, no extra logos, no clutter. The educational message must be understandable at a glance without reading the small text.
