## Preparing for Boot Removal

**What to expect:**

Boot removal usually happens around weeks 10-12 (timing varies by protocol). When you first walk without your boot, it will feel strange — your ankle is stiff, your calf is weak, and your brain isn't used to trusting that leg yet.

**Getting ready:**

- **Supportive shoes** — get a pair ready with good arch support and a firm heel counter
- **Heel lifts** — many people use 0.5-1cm heel lifts initially to reduce strain on the tendon
- **Start with short distances** — your first walks will be brief and careful

## What Shoes to Get Ready

Look for:

- **Firm heel counter** — supports the back of your ankle
- **Slight heel raise** — reduces stretch on the tendon
- **Good arch support** — helps with stability

Running shoes or supportive trainers usually work well for the first few weeks out of the boot. **Avoid flat shoes** (sandals, flip-flops, ballet flats) for now.

## The Transition Phase

The gap between boot and normal walking is bigger than most people expect. Your tendon has healed, but it's not yet strong enough for full normal activity.
