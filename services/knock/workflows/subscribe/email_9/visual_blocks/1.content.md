Around weeks 4-6, many people start preparing for physiotherapy and controlled loading. This is an exciting phase — you're moving from protection to active recovery.

Timing varies a lot by protocol — always follow your clinician/physio's guidance.

## Preparing for Physio

**What to expect:**
Physiotherapy will start with slow, controlled exercises. The early focus is usually on:

- **Safe loading** with your foot pointed down
- **Building strength** gradually
- **Improving mobility** without overstretching the tendon

Your physiotherapist will guide you through specific exercises based on your protocol and progress.

## The Safe Loading Principle

During early recovery, your tendon is still healing. Controlled, slow loading in a shortened position (foot pointed down) stimulates healing without overstretching. 

**Fast movements or stretching toes up** put dangerous forces on the tendon before it's strong enough. This is why your exercises will start slowly and build gradually.
