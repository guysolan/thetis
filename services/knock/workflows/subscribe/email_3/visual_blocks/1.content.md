Hi there,

Within a week or two, you should expect an appointment with a specialist (e.g., fracture clinic or orthopaedic department). 

Clinics are busy, and it can feel overwhelming — you might forget to ask the questions that matter most.

## Why This Appointment Matters

This is when you'll get your treatment plan confirmed — whether you're going surgical or non-surgical, what your boot protocol will be, and what to expect over the next few weeks. Having your questions ready helps you get the information you need.

## Questions to Ask Your Specialist

Write these down and bring them with you:

**About Your Diagnosis:**

- **Is this a confirmed rupture?** Do I need an ultrasound?
- **Tendon Gap Size:** "If you measured a gap (e.g., 4 cm), does it close when my foot is pointed down? What does that mean for my treatment plan?"

**About Your Treatment Protocol:**

- **Angle:** "What ankle angle should I be in (how pointed down) and for how long?"
- **Mobilisation Status:** "Am I non-weight-bearing, limited weightbearing, or unrestricted weightbearing?"
- **Physiotherapy:** "When do I start physiotherapy, and what are the early goals?"

**About Safety:**

- **Blood Thinners:** "Will I get blood thinner (VTE prophylaxis)? For how long?"
- **Red Flags:** "What symptoms should trigger urgent help?"
- **After-Hours:** "What should I do after hours if I can't reach you?"

**About Daily Life:**

- **Hygiene:** "Can anything be removed to wash/shower, or is it waterproof cover only?"
- **Work Note:** "Can you provide a fit note / sick note today?"
- **Pain Management:** "How do I manage pain at home?"
- **Activities:** "What activities should I avoid?"
