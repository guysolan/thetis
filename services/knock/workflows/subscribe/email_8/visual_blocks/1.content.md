Three weeks in, and life is usually starting to feel more manageable. 

Depending on your protocol, you may be starting to put more weight through your boot (follow your clinician's specific plan).

## Walking in Your Boot

**What to expect:**

- **Your gait will feel different** — walking in a boot changes how you move, and that's normal
- **Take it steady** — you're still healing, so don't push too hard
- **Follow your protocol** — continue with your wedge schedule and weight-bearing instructions

**Making it easier:**

- **Wear supportive shoes on your good foot** — helps balance the leg length difference caused by the boot
- **Take breaks** — your leg will get tired; that's normal
- **Elevate when you can** — still important to reduce swelling

## Hygiene and Comfort

**Sleep and washing:**

- If you're struggling to sleep in your boot or want to properly wash your foot, some patients find a lightweight splint helpful — it keeps your ankle protected at the correct angle. Our [Thetis Achilles Rupture Splint](https://thetismedical.com/achilles-rupture-splint) is designed specifically for this.
- **Keep your boot clean** — check the liner regularly and use antifungal powder if needed

## Mental health

Recovery isn't just physical. It's normal to feel frustrated or anxious. If you're struggling, talk to someone — a friend, family member, or your clinician.
