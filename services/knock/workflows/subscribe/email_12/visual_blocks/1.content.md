Pain management is one of the biggest concerns in the early days after an Achilles rupture. Understanding what's normal versus what needs immediate attention can help reduce anxiety.

## What's Normal Pain?

In the first few days, you can expect:

- **Moderate to severe pain** — this is normal, especially when moving or if the foot drops
- **Pain that improves** with elevation and rest
- **Pain that responds** to prescribed pain medication
- **Swelling and bruising** — these are normal signs of injury

## When to Contact Your Medical Team

Contact your medical team immediately if you experience:

- **Severe, unrelenting pain** that doesn't improve with medication and elevation
- **Signs of infection** — increasing redness, warmth, pus, or fever
- **Numbness or tingling** that's getting worse
- **Severe swelling** that's getting worse despite elevation
- **Chest pain or shortness of breath** — could indicate a blood clot (**medical emergency**)

## Safe Pain Management Strategies

**Elevation:**
- Keep your foot elevated above heart level as much as possible
- Use pillows or a wedge — the more comfortable you are, the more you'll elevate
- Elevate even when sleeping (if possible)

**Ice:**
- Apply ice packs wrapped in a towel for 15-20 minutes, several times a day
- Don't apply ice directly to skin
- Ice helps reduce swelling, which can help with pain

**Medication:**
- Take pain medication as prescribed by your medical team
- Don't wait until pain is severe — stay ahead of it
- If medication isn't working, contact your medical team
