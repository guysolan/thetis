## Where You Are Now

Around week 10, many people are:

- **Out of the boot** (or getting close — timing varies)
- **Starting to walk** more normally
- **Building strength** through physiotherapy
- **Feeling more confident** in your recovery

This is a good time to zoom out and see how far you've come.

## Focus Areas for the Next Phase

**Keep building gradually:**

- **Strength and balance** — these take time to rebuild
- **Walking properly** — focus on heel-to-toe pattern, not rushing
- **Following your rehab plan** — consistency matters more than speed

**Avoid rushing:**

- **Don't jump back into high-impact activity** too soon
- **Listen to your body** — some stiffness and swelling is normal
- **Trust the process** — recovery takes time, but you're on the right track

## The Long-Term View

Recovery from an Achilles rupture is a marathon, not a sprint. The middle phase (where you are now) is about rebuilding strength and function. The later phases are about returning to sport and preventing rerupture.

Each phase has its own goals and challenges. You're doing well.
