### Day 1 - Protection (Email 2)

In these early days, protection is everything.

The golden rule: keep your foot pointed down (plantarflexion). This keeps the tendon ends together.

Assume you are non-weightbearing unless your clinician specifically told you otherwise. Use your crutches and move slowly.

Check your email for the 'urgent help' checklist (red flags like new calf swelling or chest pain).

- Thetis

---

### Day 2 - Specialist Appointment (Email 3)

Got a specialist appointment coming up?

Make sure to ask about: gap size, boot angle, weight-bearing rules, and when physio starts.

For a full week-by-week plan, check out our Recovery Course: https://thetismedical.com/course

- Thetis

---

### Day 3 - Essentials (Email 4)

Week 1 essentials: a leg elevation wedge, shower stool, and a small backpack or bum bag.

A bag is essential for carrying things while you're on crutches. Check your email for more tips on home setup.

- Thetis

---

### Day 5 - Treatment Decision (Email 5)

Surgery vs. Non-surgical: both can lead to great outcomes.

The most important thing is following the right protocol. Make sure you know your tendon gap size and your specific physio timeline.

More details on treatment paths are in your email.

- Thetis

---

### Day 7 - Boot Basics (Email 6)

Boot basics: your boot keeps your tendon safe 24/7.

Follow your wedge schedule exactly and never adjust it without instruction.

If you're struggling to sleep in the 'big boot', you can try the Thetis Splint: https://thetismedical.com/splint

- Thetis

---

### Week 1 - Routine (Email 7)

In Week 1, swelling and bruising are normal.

Keep elevating and protecting that tendon 24/7. Check your email for 'Red Flags' (like calf swelling or chest pain) that mean you should seek urgent help.

You're doing the right things.

- Thetis

---

### Week 3 - Walking (Email 8)

Three weeks in!

Walking in the boot will feel a bit strange at first, which is completely normal. Take it steady, wear a supportive shoe on your good foot, and keep elevating when you can.

Check your email for tips on sleep and washing.

- Thetis

---

### Week 5 - Physio Prep (Email 9)

Time to start preparing for physio.

The early focus is on safe, slow loading with your foot pointed down. Avoid any fast movements or stretching your toes up.

Our Recovery Course includes illustrated guides for this stage: https://thetismedical.com/course

- Thetis

---

### Week 8 - Post-Boot (Email 10)

Thinking about life after the boot? Removal usually happens around weeks 10-12.

Start looking for supportive shoes and heel lifts now. Your first walks will be brief and careful—it’s a big transition.

Full guide to life after the boot is in your email.

- Thetis

---

### Week 10 - Check-in (Email 11)

Week 10: you’ve come a long way!

Focus on building your strength and balance gradually. Resist the urge to jump back into high-impact activities too soon.

Progress can feel slow, but you’re on the right track. More support is at thetismedical.com.

- Thetis

---

### Pain Management (Email 12)

Managing pain: moderate pain that improves with elevation is normal.

Contact your team immediately if you have unrelenting pain, signs of infection, or any chest pain/breathlessness.

A full guide to pain management is in your email.

- Thetis

---

### Returning to Work (Email 13)

Thinking about returning to work?

Desk jobs often take 2-4 weeks (with elevation), while physical roles usually need 8-12+ weeks. Talk to your employer early about modified duties.

Check your email for our return-to-work checklist.

- Thetis

---

### Rerupture Prevention (Email 14)

Rerupture prevention: your tendon is healing but not yet fully strong.

Avoid any sudden movements or stretching your toes up. Check your email for warning signs (like a sudden 'pop') and how to stay safe during this phase.

- Thetis
