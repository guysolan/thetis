If you are asking **when to start scar massage after Achilles surgery**, the practical answer is: start only when the wound is **fully closed**, with no open areas, no weeping, and no remaining scabs, and after your team confirms it is safe. For many patients, that decision happens around the 2-week wound review, but timing varies.

The goal is not to "break tissue down hard." The goal is gentle, consistent scar mobility so skin and soft tissue do not stick down as you recover.

## Key Takeaways

- Start scar massage only after the wound is fully closed and your clinician says yes.
- Around 2 weeks is a common checkpoint, but not everyone is ready at the same time.
- Use light pressure and small circular movements for 5 to 10 minutes, 2 to 3 times daily.
- Consistency matters more than expensive products.
- Stop and seek review if there is wound opening, discharge, spreading redness, or rising pain.
- Scar care supports recovery, but it does not replace your boot and rehab protocol.

## When Is It Safe to Start Scar Care?

After Achilles surgery, your early priority is still tendon protection. Scar work is a later add-on, not the day-one focus. Most teams review the wound and remove sutures at about 2 weeks, then decide whether scar mobilisation can begin.

Use this simple rule:

| Wound status | Scar massage |
| --- | --- |
| Open area, scab, bleeding, or fluid | **Do not start** |
| Fully closed, dry, and reviewed by team | **Can start gently** |

If you are unsure, wait and ask. Starting a few days later is safer than starting too early on a wound that is not ready.

## Why Scar Massage Helps (and What Evidence Says)

Scar massage is commonly used to improve tissue glide, comfort, and confidence with movement. In Achilles recovery, patients often describe the scar area as tight, "stuck," or sensitive once swelling settles.

Current research on post-operative scar massage suggests potential benefits for pain and mobility, but methods are inconsistent across studies. That means scar massage is reasonable and widely used, but there is no single perfect protocol that fits everyone.

In practice, clinicians usually focus on:

- Gentle tissue mobility
- Symptom-guided pressure
- Regular, short sessions
- Progression over time rather than aggressive force

That fits the same principle used across Achilles rehab: **controlled progression beats heroics**.

## How to Do Scar Massage Safely at Home

Once cleared by your specialist or physiotherapist:

1. Wash hands and check the scar first.
2. Apply a small amount of plain moisturiser or oil to reduce friction.
3. Use **light pressure only** with one or two fingertips.
4. Move in small circles along and around the scar.
5. Continue for **5 to 10 minutes**, **2 to 3 times daily**.

You can also add gentle direction changes (up-down, side-to-side) if comfortable, but avoid sharp pain. Mild tenderness is common. Strong pain is a signal to reduce pressure.

### Practical do and do not list

| Do | Do not |
| --- | --- |
| Keep pressure light and controlled | Dig deeply into painful tissue |
| Build consistency daily | Do one aggressive session then stop for days |
| Combine with your rehab plan | Replace boot or loading rules with massage |
| Ask if uncertain | Self-progress through wound concerns |

## When to Stop and Seek Review

Stop scar massage and contact your team if you notice:

- Wound reopening
- New discharge or bad smell
- Spreading redness or heat
- Fever or feeling unwell
- Rapidly worsening pain

These may indicate infection or wound complications and need clinical review, not home self-management.

Also remember clot safety in this phase. If you develop new one-leg swelling, calf pain, or chest symptoms, follow the red-flag pathway in [Beyond the Boot: Managing Your Clot Risk](/blog/beyond-the-boot-managing-clot-risk-after-achilles-rupture).

## What This Means for Your Recovery

Scar care is useful, but timing is everything. The safest strategy is:

1. Protect first.
2. Confirm full wound closure.
3. Start gentle, consistent scar mobilisation.
4. Progress only if symptoms stay calm.

If your local protocol differs slightly, follow your own surgical team. Practice varies between units and individual wound healing speed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I start scar massage exactly at 2 weeks?

Sometimes, yes, but only if the wound is fully closed and your team confirms it. Two weeks is a common review point, not an automatic start date.

### What if there is still a scab on my incision?

Wait. Do not massage across an area with active scab or incomplete closure.

### Which cream is best for scar massage?

A simple moisturiser or oil is usually enough. Technique and consistency matter more than brand.

### Should scar massage hurt?

It can feel mildly tender, but it should not be sharply painful. Reduce pressure if pain rises.

### Does scar massage replace physiotherapy?

No. It is one part of recovery. Keep following your boot settings, loading plan, and physiotherapy schedule.
